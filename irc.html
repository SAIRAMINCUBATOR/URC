About IRC

The journey of a thousand miles begins with one step.

The International Rover Challenge (IRC) is an annual event that brings together teams of talented students, engineers, and enthusiasts from around the world to showcase their innovation and skills in designing and operating rovers. The competition aims to promote collaboration, creativity, and advancements in robotics and space exploration.

VISION

At the International Rover Challenge (IRC), our vision is to be a global catalyst for innovation and collaboration in the field of robotics and space exploration. We envision a future where passionate individuals and teams, regardless of geographical boundaries, come together to push the boundaries of technology, fostering a new era of discovery and advancement.

MISSION

The mission of the International Rover Challenge (IRC) is to cultivate a global community of innovators, scientists, and enthusiasts dedicated to advancing the frontiers of robotics and space exploration. We are committed to fostering excellence and teamwork, providing a platform where diverse teams can collaborate, share knowledge, and push the boundaries of technology. Through hands-on experiences and educational initiatives, we aspire to inspire the next generation of scientists and engineers, encouraging a passion for discovery and problem-solving. By celebrating diversity and inclusivity, we aim to create an environment that reflects the richness of perspectives within the scientific community. Our mission extends beyond the competition, seeking to contribute to the progress of space exploration, promote responsible innovation, and emphasize ethical and sustainable practices in the pursuit of scientific and technological advancements. At IRC, we believe in the power of collaboration, education, and innovation to shape the future of robotics and propel humanity toward new horizons in space.

Design, build, and operate a rover capable of exploring and conducting scientific experiments on other planets, while inspiring and educating future generations of scientists, engineers, and space enthusiasts.
